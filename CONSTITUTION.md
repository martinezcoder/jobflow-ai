# Project Principles

These principles guide every decision made in this project.

They are intentionally stable and should only change after careful consideration.

## Product First

Technology exists to serve the product.

Every technical decision should answer:

> What user problem does this solve?

## People Before Jobs

We are not building a job tracker.

We are helping people build the professional life they truly want.

## Simplicity Over Cleverness

Prefer simple, explicit solutions over clever abstractions.

Code is read far more often than it is written.

## Domain-Driven Design

Every Rails model should represent a validated business concept.

Avoid creating models simply because they seem technically convenient.

## YAGNI by Default

Do not build for hypothetical future requirements.

The product should evolve as new needs become real.

## Convention Over Configuration

Whenever Rails provides a good convention, prefer it over custom solutions.

Only introduce complexity when it clearly solves a real problem.

## AI Assists, Humans Decide

AI is a powerful collaborator, not the decision maker.

Critical product, architecture and business decisions always require human judgment.

## Small, Incremental Changes

Prefer many small improvements over large rewrites.

Every change should leave the project in a slightly better state.

## Tests Build Confidence

Tests exist to increase confidence, not to maximize coverage metrics.

Write tests where they provide meaningful value.

## Documentation Has a Purpose

Every document should have a single responsibility.

Documentation should support development, not slow it down.

## Maintainability Over Speed

We optimize for a codebase that remains understandable and enjoyable to work on years from now.

Short-term speed should never compromise long-term quality.

## Challenge Assumptions

Question ideas—including your own.

The best solution is the one that best serves the user, not the one that confirms previous decisions.
